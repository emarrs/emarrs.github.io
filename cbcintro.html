Comfort Breeds Complicity. What if the institutions selling us products and services operated with complete transparency, exposing their methods to remain competitive in hyper-globalized late capitalism? What if they were upfront about the ways they exploit both the consumer and the workers who create these products?
--> Itâ€™s in the best interest of corporations to feed our discontent with products. When our discontent is directed at the systemic injustice that allows them to prosper, they must market products that create the false sense that we are doing something about the problem, while ensuring we remain passive consumers. Each product in this imaginary catalog is inspired by a real-life example of corporate profiting off anti-corporate or anti-establishment values. Each is overtly designed to relieve or distract a consumer from the pain brought by systemic injustices, in order to dissuade them from acting radically. As long as we believe that we just need to be better consumers to make progress, no progress will be made :P
